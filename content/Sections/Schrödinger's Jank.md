[[The Storytelling of Video Game Installations]]

Further complicating the notion of jank is if jank can truly be experienced in a single-player environment. Without observation or confirmation from another party, moments in gameplay that could be seen as janky will likely be perceived as bad design or as player error. As a result, all mechanics in games have the potential of being perceived as janky. This means that our ability to detect and study jank is context-dependent.

There is, undoubtedly, a social contract that must be formed before an experience in a game can be classified as "jank" instead of as a glitch, as good/bad design, or as simply outdated.
