**Research Questions**

What is "jank," and how can developers discover it and/or leverage it for creating new ideas to use when designing games? How is jank different than "glitch" or "bug," and why does this distinction matter? Does developing a research paper as if it were a game impact the style, and are there any benefits to doing so?

**Methodology**

In order to more closely examine jank, this paper will explore the concept of videogame "challenge" runs (wherein a player designs a new game in a pre-existing system), as well as designing games for and with jank by closely interrogating the methods in which designers create digital systems. These design methodologies will rely heavily on [[Introducing Unpredictability into Design]] in order to provide developers the opportunity to organically synthesize new design paradigms.


**Examining challenge runs:**
	Choosing some challenge runs from YouTube as case studies will help to demonstrate the ability of experienced players to design their own games within systems. Examining these games will also help to demonstrate when players have identified a gap in games available within existing systems.

**Designing games with/for jank:**
	Using the challenge run case studies as a springboard for idea generation, I will design both tools and games using the principles of jank. The ideas for these games will be developed in the [[Game and Section Ideas]] section of the document. These games will investigate the use of different digital spaces, hardware, and input methods.
