p87. "Some writers mistakenly understand a game overview as a summary of the story of the game, similar to how literary or film analyses provide a synopsis of a novel or a movie... Plus reducing a game to a story misses the participation of the player as well as the systemic nature of games. While some games may have a series of events to mark the progress of the game, in other cases the story events are irrelevant to the gameplay."

p88. "The building blocks that make up the game overview are: 
- number of players; 
- rules and goals of the game/game modes; 
- game mechanics; 
- spaces of the game; 
- fictional world of the game; 
- story; 
- gameplay experience; 
- game communities."

p97. "Questions that help describe the rules and goals of the game: 
- What are the rules of the game? What are the constraints and affordances provided to the player? 
- Is there a goal in the game? Does achieving the goal end the game? 
- What are the different modes of the game? How does each mode change the core rule set of the game?"
