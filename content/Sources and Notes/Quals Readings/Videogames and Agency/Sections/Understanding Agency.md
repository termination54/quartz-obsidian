p16. "She argues that agency is better understood as ‘aesthetic pleasure’ which takes place in the mind of the person reading/ playing (ibid. 128). This approach is somewhat problematic because it conditions the experience of agency with a ‘narrative satisfaction’ (ibid. 140), thereby narrativising the entire gameplay experience. It also implies that agency can only be achieved as a positive experience, so emotions like frustration (as triggered by a challenging in-game situation) or fear (the primary component in playing horror games) are not part of it."
