Keogh, Brendan. _A Play of Bodies: How We Perceive Videogames_. The MIT Press, 2018. [https://doi.org/10.7551/mitpress/10963.001.0001](https://doi.org/10.7551/mitpress/10963.001.0001).

**Key Terms**
- Phenomenology: "an approach that concentrates on the study of consciousness and the objects of direct expression"
- Embodied Textuality: "the synthesized embodied experience of audiovisual design... that constitutes the site of meaningful engagement with the videogame" (p32)
- Essential Instability: "understanding our bodies.. as an *intercorporeal* affair continuously mediated by our ongoing interactions with other human and nonhuman bodies" (p38)
- Immersion: "being transported to and enveloped in another world discrete from the actual world" (p47)
- Proprioception: "the preconscious sense of the position of the self in space" (p56)
- Liminal State: "the state of being in-between or something that is in a state of transition"
- Ephemeral: "the concept of things being transitory; existing only briefly"
- Interruptability: "...the player can be ...interrupted with little consequence" (p82)
- Mimetic Interfaces: "...input devices that ask the player to imitate the actions being asked of their avatar on (or inferred by) the screen." (p83)
- Dressage: "training; particularly in relation to horse riding"

**Discuss Spaces:**
- [[Audiovisual]]: p22. of PDF: "Video games are not a core game wrapped in an interchangeable audiovisual skin; videogames are materially constituted *by* their audiovisuality."
- [[A Decentered Self]]: p36. of PDF: "We experience the world through our body, but we experience our own body itself more as a frontier where our 'natural ignorance of ourselves' constantly moves the body..."
- [[Consensual Hallucination]] p46/47. of PDF: "The act of videogame play itself often requires repressing a conscious consideration of the embodied experience that videogame play depends on... We must look at what the player looks through."
- [[Cybernetics]] p55. of PDF: "...conceive of videogame play as a material feedback loop of information and agency that flows between the machine and the player."
- [[Actual Virtual]] p75 of PDF: "... the phenomenon of being present in both actual and virtual worlds at once, of being simultaneously here and there... We do not simply experience the world "through" our... device, but also experience the.. device as an incorporated aspect of our world."
- [[Mimesis]] p84 of PDF: "Mimetic interfaces draw explicit attention *to* the actual world and the player's positionality... mimetic devices draw attention to the playing body incorporated in the play circuit in contrast to how traditional input devices work to draw attention *away* from the playing body."
- [[Musicality]] p154 of PDF: "playing with music as a dancer plays with music: matching bodily movements and visual spectacle to musical movements and sounds rather than to specific instruments or notes, capturing the *sensation* of music in the player-dancer's gestures."
- [[Permanence]] p173 of PDF: "Every previous death had felt so trivial, but now I feel the retrospective *wasting* of two hours of real labor and the permanent destruction of those resources like a physical, actual loss..."
- [[Gender]] p213/214 of PDF: "The history of videogames is enmeshed with the history of computing... in the late 1940s... most computers were literally women... These human computers were commonly women because the work required of them was often seen as clerical and was thus easily gendered as 'women's work...' As the role of digital computing shifted perceptually from a menial number-crunching task to one of significant mathematical importance, however, it shifted from feminized labor to masculinized labor..."
