"Video games are not a core game wrapped in an interchangeable audiovisual skin; videogames are materially constituted *by* their audiovisuality."

[[A Play of Bodies]]

**Quotes**
- p23: "videogames are looked at, listened to, and physically touched in order for players to perceive an imperfect and partial sense of presence..."
- p23: "videogame experience is *a play of bodies* that flickers between... 'me' and 'not me.'"
- p33: "videogames... blend narratives and fictions with virtual bodies and objects that are physically *felt* through hands wrapped around plastic controllers or tapping away at keyboards or smearing touchscreens."
- p46: "Videogames regularly remind players of their intangibility through nondiegetic elements, on-screen prompts, glitches, and the simple fact that they rarely, if ever, hold the player’s full attention"
- p140: "How the videogame looks (how I view), sounds (hear), and feels (touch) creates the foundation through which I perceive the videogame as a world and interpret its systems, rules, *and* themes."
- p145: "Polish effects add appeal and *emphasize the physical nature of interactions*, helping designers sell those objects to the player as real... if all polish were removed, the essential functionality of the game would be unaltered, but the player would find the experience less perceptually convincing and therefore less appealing. This is because - for players - simulation and polish are indistinguishable."
- p149: "Audiovisual representation does not just give context to videogame action; it *constructs* videogame action."
- p150: "There exists no essence, no content, of the experience of videogame play detached from the audiovisuals and haptics that produce it."
- p165: "emphasize *intensity of feeling* in order to create an experience that is in a way 'truer' than a photorealistic representation might be."
