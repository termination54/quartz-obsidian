[[A Play of Bodies]]

Most similar to [[Audiovisual]], with a focus on the movements of a dancer.

**Quotes**
- p154: "Videogame players are not attending a dance performance, They *are* the performer... and what might be ephemeral to the observer of a dance recital is embodied and felt by the dancer as the rhythms of their body incorporates the rhythms of the music."
- p162: "Just as Merleau-Ponty insists one perceives the world synaesthetically through different sensorial fields, *Audiosurf*, by translating the sensation of performing a song... into 'playing' a videogame, communicates the phenomenological sensation of engaging with that song through different sensorial fields across different embodiments of different media."
- p166/167: "*Slave of God* is exemplary of Kanaga's observation that what most defines the videogame form is presence, movement, and sensation. The 'meaningless moments' Kirkpatrick identifies in dance are literalized here as the player is caught up with sights and sounds to stagger around and be overwhelmed by the nightclub... Yet the sensorial engagement of its sights and sounds are not connected solely to what I am able to 'do' in this world in a strictly configurable sense. There is something innately meaningful in my direct engagement with these eye-searing colors and repetitive, droning beats."
- p189: "Even if after the fact the player suppresses those failed temporal strands as inauthentic, those strands remain part of the live performance, influence future plays of the videogame..."
