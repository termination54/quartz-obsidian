I am interested in the subject of jank because jank provides an opportunity for all players to design their own games within digital systems. The issue I see as needing investigation is how to find and utilize jank because its application in the design process has not yet been explored. I am particularly well suited to researching this issue because I have an acute interest in early 2000s games that are often seen as janky, and I have the skillset to design and develop my own digital games. So in this proposal I will explore early 2000s games and videogame challenge runs to develop games and software to assist developers in "janking" their designs and games.

The key context(s) which need taking account of when researching this doctorate are:
- Understanding industry-standard UX design because I need to be able to pinpoint moments in design where existing games break that mold (intentionally or unintentionally).
- Techniques developers have used to autoethnographically research their own design processes because I need to develop my own methods and put them into place during the development of the games designed for this dissertation.

The main literature relevant to my thesis is:
- [[Questions Over Answers Reflective Game Design]]
- [[A Play of Bodies]]
- [[The Gamer's Brain How Neuroscience and UX can Impact Video Game Design]]
- [[Challenge Runs]]
- [[Jank Accounts We Should Study ‘Broken’ Games]]

Knowledge of this literature is important to my thesis because:
- I will be able to put in place a methodology for researching my own design process, as well as using the concepts discussed to design my own methodologies.
- I will be able to better understand the concept of a videogame, contexts in which videogames can be researched, and methods for understanding games beyond the software in which they are played.
- Understanding industry-standard applications of UX design will enable my own projects to experiment with breaking those notions and being able to better understand when existing games do the same.
- Demonstrating how jank has already been used in online gaming spaces to design games and examining some of the games in both existing videogames and games scholarship that challenge runs identify will help strengthen my own games.
- Analyzing the lenses in which jank has already been examined will strengthen my own definition of jank and provide me with additional avenues to explore jank.

The most useful evidence to gather for this project then would be through analyzing challenge runs and autoethnographically recording a "janked" design process because it would allow me to investigate existing instances of jank as well as exploring new avenues that present themselves as a result of the methodology I develop. I would try to ensure it is robust evidence by ensuring I have a decent to deep knowledge of the software the runner designed a challenge run within as well as applying MDM (or my own modified version) during the design process.

The most appropriate methodological approach to adopt in investigating my topic are applying the principles in [[Introduction to Game Analysis]] to the games designed by challenge runners and applying MDM to my own design process because I will be able to develop detailed case studies on challenge runs to better exemplify how challenge runs apply jank to create new games and I will have a detailed history of my thought process throughout the design process of each "janked" game I design. And within this general approach, the best methods to use are videogame formalism because the principles therein are useful for analyzing the game being playing within a digital system, rather than simply analyzing the "[[Videogame Formalism On Form, Aesthetic Experience, and Methodology|game's self-valuable material]]."

In my thesis I want to investigate jank through the lens of it being an opportunity to players to design their own games within systems. I want to then use these findings to develop a few pieces of software that will assist developers to experience this line of thinking during the design process.
